Cerebrotendinous xanthomatosis (CTX) is among the few inherited neurometabolic 
disorders amenable to specific treatment. It is easily diagnosed using plasma 
cholestanol. We wished to delineate the natural history of the most common 
neurological and non-neurological symptoms in thirteen patients with CTX. 
Diarrhea almost always developed within the first year of life. Cataract and 
school difficulties usually occurred between 5 and 15 years of age preceding by 
years the onset of motor or psychiatric symptoms. The median age at diagnosis 
was 24.5 years old. It appears critical to raise awareness about CTX among 
paediatricians in order to initiate treatment before irreversible damage occurs.
