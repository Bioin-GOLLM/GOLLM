Switching the N-Capping Region from all-L to all-D Amino Acids in a VEGF Mimetic 
Helical Peptide.

The N-capping region of an α-helix is a short N-terminal amino acid stretch that 
contributes to nucleate and stabilize the helical structure. In the VEGF mimetic 
helical peptide QK, the N-capping region was previously demonstrated to be a key 
factor of QK helical folding. In this paper, we explored the effect of the 
chiral inversion of the N-capping sequence on QK folding, performing 
conformational analysis in solution by circular dichroism and NMR spectroscopy. 
The effect of such a modification on QK stability in serum and the proliferative 
effect were also evaluated.
