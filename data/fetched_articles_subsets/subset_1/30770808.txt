Recessive mutations in muscle-specific isoforms of FXR1 cause congenital 
multi-minicore myopathy.

FXR1 is an alternatively spliced gene that encodes RNA binding proteins (FXR1P) 
involved in muscle development. In contrast to other tissues, cardiac and 
skeletal muscle express two FXR1P isoforms that incorporate an additional 
exon-15. We report that recessive mutations in this particular exon of FXR1 
cause congenital multi-minicore myopathy in humans and mice. Additionally, we 
show that while Myf5-dependent depletion of all FXR1P isoforms is neonatal 
lethal, mice carrying mutations in exon-15 display non-lethal myopathies which 
vary in severity depending on the specific effect of each mutation on the 
protein.
