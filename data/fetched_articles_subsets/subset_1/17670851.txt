Longitudinal myelitis in patient with systemic lupus erythematosus, homozygous 
prothrombin G20210A and heterozygous MTHFR 677T.

Touma Z(1), Atweh SF, Kibbi L, Arayssi T.

Author information:
(1)Department of Internal Medicine, Division of Rheumatology, American 
University of Beirut Medical Center, New York, NY 10017-2303, USA.

Longitudinal myelitis is an uncommon complication of systemic lupus 
erythematosus (SLE). We describe an unusual case of longitudinal myelitis and 
ischemic stroke in the presence of homozygous prothrombin G20210A, heterozygous 
MTHFR 677T mutations and the absence of antiphospholipid antibodies in a young 
woman with SLE.
