Combining site-specific mutagenesis and seeding as a strategy to crystallize 
'difficult' proteins: the case of Staphylococcus aureus thioredoxin.

Roos G(1), Brosens E, Wahni K, Desmyter A, Spinelli S, Wyns L, Messens J, Loris 
R.

Author information:
(1)Brussels Center for Redox Biology, Vlaams Interuniversitair Instituut voor 
Biotechnologie at the Vrije Universiteit Brussel, Pleinlaan 2, 1050 Brussel, 
Belgium.

The P31T mutant of Staphylococcus aureus thioredoxin crystallizes spontaneously 
in space group P2(1)2(1)2(1), with unit-cell parameters a = 41.7, b = 49.5, c = 
55.6 A. The crystals diffract to 2.2 A resolution. Isomorphous crystals of 
wild-type thioredoxin as well as of other point mutants only grow when seeded 
with the P31T mutant. These results suggest seeding as a valuable tool 
complementing surface engineering for proteins that are hard to crystallize.
